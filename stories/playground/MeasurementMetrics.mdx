# Measuring What Matters

Three questions drive all measurement on Spaceship:

1. **What did the user come to do?** — Intent
2. **Did the AI artifacts match what they expected?** — Output quality
3. **Did the user get what they came for?** — Resolution

---

## The Three User Intents

Every session maps to one of three modes. These define what "success" looks like for that session.

| Intent | The real job | Success looks like |
|---|---|---|
| **Validate** | Test an idea / build evidence for a tender or decision | Something shareable that helps them decide or convince |
| **Explore** | Understand what the platform can do | Feels oriented — may or may not build |
| **Build** | Make a working app for personal or team use | App is used after the session ends |

---

## Part 1 — Intent

Intent is captured as the **first Q&A card** in every session. Compulsory, single-tap.

**Question:** *"What are you here to do today?"*

- Test or validate an idea
- Explore what's possible
- Build something specific

**If Validate is selected**, show one follow-up:

*"Are you validating a problem or a solution?"*

- Validating a problem
- Validating a solution

This sub-type matters because the artifact a user needs is different in each case — problem validation produces a demo to surface pain, solution validation produces a prototype to pitch an approach before tendering.

> Intent drives everything downstream — which resolution questions get asked, what security posture applies, and how the final artifact is framed.

---

## Part 2 — Output Quality

Every AI-generated artifact in the flow has a quality gate. The gate appears inline below the artifact after it renders.

### Rating format

**3-point scale** for all quality gates — not stars, not binary.

The "partial" state is critical. A user who says "needs edits" is telling us the Q&A didn't extract the right information. A user who says "missed the mark" is telling us something broke earlier. You lose that distinction with yes/no.

The only exception is the Security Review — which is an **acknowledgement gate**, not a quality gate.

---

### Quality gates by artifact

**Project Brief**
> *"Does this brief capture what you had in mind?"*
> - Yes, looks right
> - Needs some edits
> - This missed the mark

What failure tells us: the Q&A questions weren't extracting the right information from the user.

---

**Options Card**
> *"Were these options useful?"*
> - Yes
> - Somewhat
> - Not really

What failure tells us: the options generated don't match the user's intent type.

On negative: optional free-text — *"What was missing?"*

---

**Security Review — Acknowledgement Gate**

Users are not qualified to evaluate security quality. What matters is whether they **consciously understood and accepted** the risks.

> *"Do you understand and accept the security risks outlined above?"*
> - Yes, I accept and will proceed
> - I need to check with my team first

"I need to check with my team" is a real workflow state in a govt context — not a no. The build should pause and hold until they return and accept.

---

**Prototype**
> *"Does this match what you described?"*
> - Yes, looks right
> - Partially
> - Not really

What failure tells us: the brief to build translation failed somewhere.

On negative: optional free-text — *"What didn't match?"*

---

## Part 3 — Resolution

Resolution signals are captured at three moments. The moments are fixed — every session hits them. What changes per intent is the question asked.

---

### Moment A — At prototype reveal

Intent-aware. The question changes based on what the user said they came to do.

| Intent | Question |
|---|---|
| Validate | *"Do you have enough to make a decision or present this?"* |
| Explore | *"Did you find what you were looking for?"* |
| Build | *"Is this ready to use or share with your team?"* |

3-point scale: Yes / Getting there / Not quite

On negative: optional free-text — *"What got in the way?"*

---

### Moment B — At session close

Same question for all intents.

> *"Did you get what you came for?"*
> - Yes, got what I needed
> - Partially — still in progress
> - Not quite

On negative: optional free-text — *"What got in the way?"*

---

### Moment C — 7 days post-session

Delivered via email or surfaced in-chat on next login. Multi-select — multiple outcomes can be true.

> *"How did it go with [Project Name]?"*
> - Presented or shared it
> - Helped us make a decision
> - Still working on it
> - Sparked a new idea
> - Didn't end up using it

---

## Implicit Signals

Captured passively — no user action or prompting required.

### Sharing and export

These are the strongest resolution indicators. If someone shares or exports, the session almost certainly delivered value.

| Signal | What it tells us |
|---|---|
| Prototype link shared | High confidence the session resolved — they have something worth showing |
| Artifact copied | Using the content outside the platform |
| Artifact downloaded | Taking it somewhere — into a workflow or presentation |

### Behavioural signals

| Signal | What it tells us |
|---|---|
| Re-query within 5 mins of an output | Resolution failure — the answer didn't land |
| Session stall (15+ mins no input) | Stuck or lost — friction point in the flow |
| Returned to session the next day | Unresolved but still engaged |
| Collaborators joined | Team utility — strong signal for Build intent |
| Returned with a second user | Demoing to a stakeholder — strong Validate signal |

### Downstream signals

These connect session activity to real-world outcomes without asking the user to report anything manually.

| Signal | How it's collected |
|---|---|
| Follow-up chat referencing the same project | Detect same project name or topic in subsequent sessions |
| Email thread about the project | Detect project references in outbound or inbound email |
| New session spawned from the same brief | Detect if a new session reuses or extends prior session output |

These matter most for Validate intent — where the real outcome (a tender being raised, a decision being made) happens entirely outside Spaceship.

## Who Sees This Data

| Signal type | Audience |
|---|---|
| Intent, quality gates, resolution prompts | User-facing — collected through interaction |
| Implicit signals (share, copy, stall, re-query) | Platform-level — invisible to user |
| Session Health Dashboard | Spaceship team only — surfaces where agent prompts are underperforming, where users stall, which artifacts consistently fail quality gates |

The Session Health Dashboard is an internal tool for the team to know what to improve in the agent — not something users see or interact with.
